Forcible Wedlock

Forced marriage is a human rights violation, affecting women who often face harassment and discrimination. Mimes, a nonverbal communication tool, can raise awareness about forced marriage and women's harassment. Through expressive gestures, movements, and facial expressions, mimes portray emotional conflicts, power imbalances, and lack of agency, emphasizing the urgent need for combat.

Torn Veil: A dramatic image of a torn bridal veil caught on a tree branch, symbolizing the loss of self-determination and freedom.

Range

Mirror Reflections: The script mimics a forced kiss situation using a mirror image, creating a surreal and thought-provoking duality image.

Masked Faces: White-masked mime artists express grief, resistance, and entanglement, echoing the emotions associated with this concept.

Bird cage: Representing the birds in the cage, representing the desire for freedom and the struggle to break free from the constraints of society.
